SIZING GUIDE

How to Measure

Two minutes with a soft tape measure saves you weeks. Every piece is made to your measurements. Get it right and it'll fit perfect out of the box.

Radio Strap Belt Helmet Band

Radio Strap

Measuring can be done with an existing radio strap or webbing/string.

Using an existing radio strap – adjust strap to desired length and measure from top of one trigger snap to top of second trigger snap. This will be your overall length.

Using webbing or string – place the beginning of the string where you would like to have your radio holster sit. Take other end of the webbing/string across your body, over your shoulder and back to the beginning of the string. This will be your overall length.

Belt

Belt sizing isn't the same as pants size: pants are cut size, belts are worn size. Always measure, never guess.

Use a belt that already fits – lay it flat and measure from where the leather meets the buckle to the hole you use most. That number is your size.

No belt that fits? – measure your waist where you wear the belt, through the belt loops, snug but not tight.

Round up to the nearest inch – our belts come with 5 holes spaced 1 inch apart, so you'll have room either way.

Helmet Band

Helmet bands wrap the front brim of your structural fire helmet. Most helmets fit a standard size, but we make every band to spec.

Tell us your helmet size by measuring around the helmet – Or tell us your helmet model; Cairns 1010, Cairns N5A, Phenix TL-2, Bullard PX, etc. We've fit them all.

If you have a current band that fits – measure it tip to tip.

Tell us what you want stamped on it – last name, station number, rank, badge. Whatever you want stamped.

Still not sure? Send us your measurements and a photo of what you're carrying, and we'll figure it out together.
